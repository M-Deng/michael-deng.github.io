About Me

My name is Michael Deng, and I was raised in Calgary, Alberta, Canada. After completing my senior year at Western Canada High School, I left the cold Alberta plains for the sunny West Coast to attend the University of California, Berkeley. I have always been immensely interested in technology, and I love the culture of the Bay Area. I am currently pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. You can reach me through email at any time here!

Experience

I am currently working on an undergraduate research project that uses web-scraping and machine learning to collect and analyze data sets on a wide range of companies. I am also a Technology Director of the Alpha Kappa Psi professional organization in charge of maintaining and updating our website. I am proficient in Java, Python, C, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. In my free time, I am always working on cool side projects. Check them out at my Github!

Projects

In Java, I developed two puzzle games, Connecting Dots and Sliding Blocks, utilizing multiple data structures and object-oriented programming, and a Huffman Encoding lossless compression software.

In Python, I designed a dice game, a tower defense game (similar to Plants vs. Zombies), a Twitter trends analyzer and geographic visualizer, and a Scheme programming language interpreter.

In C and MIPS, I built an image-processing program that generates depth maps from stereo images, compresses and decompresses the depth maps, and creates 3D autostereograms from the depth maps.
